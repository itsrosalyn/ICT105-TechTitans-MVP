# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title

QR Code Attendance System with GPS Verification

## Target Users Studied

* ICT Students (Rangsit University)
* ICT Lecturers (Rangsit University)

## Number of Responses / Interviews

21 total respondents

* 18 ICT students
* 3 ICT lecturers

## Main Repeated Problem

Attendance checking consumes valuable lecture time, and current attendance methods allow proxy attendance and require manual management.

## Main Current Workarounds

* Verbal roll calls
* Paper sign-in sheets
* Spreadsheet attendance tracking

## Strongest Evidence Found

14 respondents reported that attendance checking takes too much class time, while 11 respondents identified proxy attendance as a recurring issue. Lecturers confirmed that manual attendance processing increases workload and reduces teaching efficiency.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings

Some respondents were uncertain about the effectiveness of GPS verification in all situations, and further validation is needed regarding location accuracy and user acceptance.

## What We Must Revise

The team should further validate GPS verification requirements and ensure the system remains simple, reliable, and user-friendly while effectively preventing proxy attendance.

## Updated Problem Statement

ICT students and lecturers at Rangsit University rely on manual or semi-digital attendance methods that consume lecture time, increase lecturer workload, and allow proxy attendance. These methods do not provide reliable real-time attendance tracking, creating inefficiencies and reducing the accuracy of attendance records. A faster and more secure attendance process is needed to improve classroom management and attendance reliability.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings

There is limited evidence regarding whether all lecturers would adopt a QR-code-based solution. Some concerns were raised about internet connectivity and preventing proxy attendance.

## What We Must Revise

Further investigate security and anti-proxy attendance features. Collect more feedback from lecturers regarding system adoption and usability.
